# Information Security Policy

**Owner:** VP of Security (CISO) · **Review cadence:** annually and after major change
· **Last reviewed:** current fiscal year.

## Purpose and scope

This policy establishes how Sample Inc. protects the confidentiality, integrity, and
availability of company and customer information. It applies to all employees,
contractors, systems, and third parties that access Sample Inc. information or
information systems.

## Governance

The information security program is led by the CISO and overseen by a monthly
Security Committee that includes Engineering, IT, Legal, and People Operations. The
program is aligned to the AICPA Trust Services Criteria and ISO/IEC 27001. Risks are
tracked in a central risk register, rated by likelihood and impact, and reviewed
quarterly by leadership.

## Policy statements

- **Least privilege.** Access to systems and data is granted on a need-to-know basis
  and reviewed at least quarterly.
- **Data classification.** Information is classified as Public, Internal,
  Confidential, or Restricted; handling requirements scale with sensitivity.
- **Encryption.** Data is encrypted in transit and at rest (see the Data Encryption
  Policy).
- **Acceptable use.** Company systems are for authorized business use; users must not
  disable security controls or install unapproved software.
- **Incident reporting.** Suspected security incidents must be reported immediately
  to security@sample.example via the documented process.
- **Exceptions.** Policy exceptions require CISO approval, are time-bound, and are
  tracked in the risk register.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
Policies are reviewed at least annually, and all staff acknowledge them on hire and
during annual security training.
